  1. Parliament Building

    It is not only the biggest building of any of the villages, but it is the centre of village life and the basis of the island and national governance. It was constructed to look like a local canoe from the front and from above. Its architecture also mimics the style of traditional Kiribati huts.

  2. Sacred Heart Cathedral

    Like most churches on the island, the Sacred Heart Cathedral is located adjacent to the main road at Teaoraereke so you won't miss it when roaming by bike, car, bus, etc.! It is managed by the Roman Catholic Church on the island and is situated close the church's headquarters and admin buildings.

  3. Ambo Island

    One of several islands comprising Tawara, Ambo has a beautiful beach and is home to the famous Ambo Lagoon Club, with swimming, volleyball and other activities.

  4. Kiribati National Library And Archives

    Archives collect and preserve original documents of organizations such as churches or governments. Libraries generally collect published sources such as books, maps, and microfilm.

  5. Aranuka Island

    Aranuka has white sandy beaches and blue waters, and is the only island in Kiribati to have tall mangrove trees with heights of more than 15 m (49 ft). The uninhabited islets of the island also provide swimming and snorkeling spots for visitors.

  6. Makin Island

    Makin is a lagoon-less small coral island which is divided into five islets by shallow ocean passages. The island resembles a large exclamation mark, with the largest islet, Makin, located in the northernmost tip of the island.

  7. Terau Travel Kiribati

    From exploring the mesmerizing coral atolls to immersing yourself in the vibrant culture of the local communities, our knowledgeable guides will accompany you on a journey of discovery. Dive into the crystal-clear waters, witness the breathtaking marine life, and connect with the warm-hearted people of Kiribati.

  8. Tabuaeran

    One of the most unique features of Tabuaeran Island is its rich cultural heritage. The local community is known for their warm hospitality and deep connection to their ancestral roots. Immerse yourself in the traditional customs and rituals of the Kiribati people for a truly authentic experience.
