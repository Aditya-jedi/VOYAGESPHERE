  1. Jellyfish Lake

    Palau's Jellyfish Lake (known in Palauan as Ongeim'l Tketau) is a saltwater lake on Mecherchar (Eil Malk) Island within Palau's Rock Islands that is known worldwide for its profusion of bright orange, harmless, and beautiful jellyfish.

  2. Milky Way

    Milky Way Palau: A Lagoon with White Clay Mud Believed to Have Healing Powers. Palau's Milky Way is a natural wonder that draws visitors from all over the world. The Milky Way is a lagoon located in the Rock Islands of Palau, a Micronesian country.

  3. Long Beach

    Long Beach is an impressive and beautiful sand bar attached to as Rock Island, located in the southern Rock Islands, not far from Carp Island and Peleliu Island. You can only reach it by boat; there are a few tours who organize that trip in Koror.

  4. Ngardmau Falls

    One of Babeldaob's premier attractions, Ngardmau Waterfall flows from Palau's tallest peak, 217m-high Mt Ngerchelchuus. It's the tallest waterfall in Micronesia. It can be accessed on foot a tough but rewarding 20- to 30-minute hike along a jungle path.

  5. Rock Island

    The Rock Islands Southern Lagoon consists of numerous large and small forested limestone islands, scattered within a marine lagoon protected by a barrier reef. The property lies within Koror State, immediately to the south of Palau's main volcanic island Babeldaob in the western Pacific Ocean.

  6. Kayangel Island

  7. Airai Bai

    Palau's oldest bai (men's meeting house) is over 100 years old, 21m long and 6m wide, with a steeply pitched roof reaching a height of 12m. It was constructed using local materials of wood and thatch on a stone platform. A number of legendary scenes and symbolic motifs are painted inside and outside.

  8. Koror

    Beneath the surface of the sea, dazzling coral gardens teem with iridescent fish, and underwater cliffs drop thousands of feet in the horizontal distance of a few paces. Inside the area sheltered by these tiny isles, calm seas afford ideal sites for water sports.
