  1. Acropolis

    The Acropolis of Athens is an ancient citadel located on a rocky outcrop above the city of Athens, Greece, and contains the remains of several ancient buildings of great architectural and historical significance, the most famous being the Parthenon.

  2. Parthenon

    The Parthenon is a former temple on the Athenian Acropolis, Greece, that was dedicated to the goddess Athena. Its decorative sculptures are considered some of the high points of classical Greek art, and the Parthenon is considered an enduring symbol of Ancient Greece, democracy, and Western civilization.

  3. Elafonissi Beach

    Elafonisi Beach is a true treasure on the southwestern side of Crete, only 5km away from the southwesternmost end of the island. It is famous for its unique pink sand, whose color was created by the thousands of tiny parts of shattered seashells.

  4. Plaka

    Plaka is the oldest and most beautiful neighborhood in Athens. Clustered around the slopes of the Acropolis, it incorporates labyrinthine streets and neoclassical architecture. Plaka is built on top of the residential part of ancient Athens.

  5. Meteora

    The Meteora is a rock formation in the regional unit of Trikala, in Thessaly, in northwestern Greece, hosting one of the most prominent complexes of Eastern Orthodox monasteries, viewed locally as second in importance only to Mount Athos.

  6. Village of Lindos

    There are many interesting things to do in Lindos, a small seaside town on the east coast of the Greek island of Rhodes. It's most well known for being home to the grand hilltop archaeological site known as the Lindos Acropolis.

  7. The Palace of Knossos

    Knossos is a Bronze Age archaeological site in Crete. The site was a major centre of the Minoan civilization and is known for its association with the Greek myth of Theseus and the minotaur. It is located on the outskirts of Heraklion, and remains a popular tourist destination.

  8. Amoudi Bay

    The history of Ammoudi dates back to the Venetian Republic and the first references to it date back to the early 13th century. In 1956, the whole area was affected by a devastating earthquake, but has since been reconstructed back to its original form. Ammoudi Beach is accessible from the top of the town of Oia.

  9. Panathenaic Stadium

    One of the main historic attractions of Athens, it is the only stadium in the world built entirely of marble. A stadium was built on the site of a simple racecourse by the Athenian statesman Lykourgos (Lycurgus) c. 400 BC, primarily for the Panathenaic Games.

  10. Hiking Trail Fira-Oia

    The hike from Fira to Oia is a must do on any Santorini itinerary. This 10km walk along the edge of the caldera is one of the most spectacular short hikes anywhere in the world.
