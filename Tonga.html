  1. Anahulu Cave

    ongatapu's most famous cave is an over-loved, slightly eerie place full of stalactites and stalagmites, and blackened from the soot of flaming-frond torches and too much traffic. Inside is an underground freshwater pool where you can swim.

  2. Talamahu Markets

    Tongan society is guided by four core values, all of which combine to ensure a genuine welcome to visitors to the Kingdom.

  3. Mt Talau

    Mt. Talau National Park is a geographical landmark in Vava'u just two kilometres from the main town of Neiafu located on the island of Vava'u in Tonga. 131 metres high, it is a limestone plateau that overlooks Neiafu, the Port of Refuge and the Vava'u archipelago.

  4. Free Church Of Tonga

    The Free Church of Tonga (also called the Centennial Church) is not just a local house of worship; it's also the name of a religious denomination. The religion was established in 1885 by King George Tupou I and the missionary Reverend Shirley Waldemar Baker to break from Australia's Methodist church.

  5. St.Joseph's Cathedral

    The main cathedral on Vavau is the St Joseph's Cathedral, a beautiful whitewashed Polynesian-style church standing on a high ground above the harbor and visible from many points in town. Additional steps and staircases lead even higher from the street to the church.

  6. Centenary Church

    The Centenary Chapel is a historic church located in Nuku'alofa, the capital city of Tonga. It was built in 1882 to commemorate the arrival of Christianity in Tonga and to celebrate the centennial anniversary of the establishment of the Free Wesleyan Church of Tonga.

  7. 3 Headed Coconut

    Behold, the three headed coconut. The only tree of it's like in the South Pacific, there is not one, but three heads to this coconut tree! This is a must see when visiting Tonga. Don't fret, you won't miss out on this as it is signposted from 10km away in the centre of Nuku'alofa!

  8. Eua National Park

    It is the country's only national forest park and covers an area of 4.51 km2 (2 sq mi). The park is located on the east coast of the island and covers an 800 metres (2,625 ft) band of tropical rainforest and is bordered on the west by sheer cliffs to the ocean, which have numerous caves and chasms.
