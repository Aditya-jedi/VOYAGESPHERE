  1. Ilulissat Icefjord

    Located on the west coast of Greenland, 250 km north of the Arctic Circle, Greenland's Ilulissat Icefjord is a tidal fjord covered with floating brash and massive ice, as it is situated where the Sermeq Kujalleq glacier calves ice into the sea. In winter, the area is frozen solid.Ilulissat is the most popular tourist destination in Greenland, home to enormous icebergs and the UNESCO World Heritage Listed Ilulissat Icefjord, and gateway to fabulous Disko Bay.

  4. Prince Christian Sound

    Spanning approximately 60 miles, it separates the mainland of Greenland from the southernmost islands in the country. One of the most striking features of Prince Christian Sound is its dramatic landscapes. Towering granite cliffs rise majestically from the water, creating an awe-inspiring sight for visitors.

  5. Sermermiut

    The area has a large and varied animal and marine life. The combination of the Ice Sheet's fresh water and the salty water of the sea offer fantastic possibilities for food. Sermermiut was excavated in 1952 and 1983, where evidence of many different cultures were found.

  7. Nuuk Cathedral

    Nuuk Cathedral (Greenlandic: Annaassisitta Oqaluffia) or Church of Our Saviour (Danish: Vor Frelser Kirke) is a wooden Lutheran cathedral in the Old Nuuk neighborhood of Nuuk, the capital of Greenland. It was established in 1849. The red building with its spire is a prominent site on the landscape.

  8. Nuuk Art Museum

    Nuuk Art Museum is the larger of only two Greenlandic Art Museums. It's located in the center of Nuuk. The museum has a collection of over 1200 items and a large wing for changing exhibitions. The collection contains works by both local artist and foreign artist depicting Greenland.

  9. Qornok

    Qornoq is an old village the Nuuk fjord system. The main part of the population left the village in the 1960'ies. It has since turned into a popular travel spot for the Nuuk population. Qornoq lies at the mouth of the icefjord, and therefore it sometimes is surrounded by icebergs.

  10. Katuaq

    The Katuaq Cultural Center is a hub for cultural experiences, where you can enjoy local dishes at Cafetuaq, attend concerts, conferences, film screenings, and much more. Over the years, Katuaq has developed a range of annual recurring events with varied and exciting content. This has made Katuaq one of the key pillars for cultural experiences in Greenland, as well as a central focal point for many of the major annual events that span the entire country.
